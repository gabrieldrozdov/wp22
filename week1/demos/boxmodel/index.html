Websites are like boxes within boxes within boxes of information. What do those boxes look like?

Let’s look at a Div

Divs have margins, padding, and borders.

This Div has:

  • 100px of margins on all sides (outside of the border)
  • 100px of padding on all sides (inside of the border)
  • 100px border on all sides (between the padding and margin)

Here's another Div

You can also set each side individually for margins, padding, and border. This leads to *funky business*.

Here's a third Div

Additionally, you can set a Div’s width and height. You can also give it a background color!

Here's a fourth Div

Normally, the padding and border are added onto the element’s width and height. If you change the "box-sizing" property to "border-box", padding and border are included in the width and height.

Margin defines the area around an element. Two elements can share the same margin space. This means that if two elements have a margin of 100px, the space between them will be 100px, not 200px.

This means that this element is only 100px away from the previous one.

Two elements can’t share the same padding space. This means that if two elements have padding of 100px, the space between them will be 200px.

This means that this element is 200px away from the previous one.

One more way to stylize this so-called box is to give it a border radius!

Border radius affects the background color of an element, too.
