One of the most excited parts of websites is that they can *change*. So far, everything has been fairly static. Let’s make it a little more dynamic, shall we?

Let’s take this paragraph for instance. Maybe I want it so that when you mouse over a specific word, that word changes size. Easy! We’ll use hover states. Try mousing over the word "easy" from before to see what I mean.

Hover states are typically used when denoting links on a page. Using a single class, you can specify how the element will look when it’s in its default state, when you mouse over it, and when you press down on it. Here’s an example!
